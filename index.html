The Practice Framework

The Four Habits That Produced Every Result I Have.

I committed to these four principles the summer between 7th and 8th grade. They require no special equipment, no expensive lessons, and no prior talent — just consistent intention. I share them with every student I work with.

```
01

Consistent Daily Practice ⏰

I practiced every single day that summer — no exceptions for weekends, travel, or days when I didn't feel motivated. Short, focused sessions done consistently will always outperform long, infrequent ones. Here is what I recommend by grade:

🌟 5th Grade: 10 min/day
💚 6th Grade: 15 min/day
💙 7th Grade: 20 min/day
⭐ 8th Grade: 20–25 min/day
02

Address Weaknesses First 🎯

Every session, I started with the hardest thing on my list — not the most comfortable. Students naturally gravitate toward what they can already do well. Real, measurable improvement lives in the spots you've been avoiding. I assigned myself one problem area per week and didn't move forward until it was solved.

03

Record Every Session 📱

I recorded every single practice session on my phone — even the bad ones. Listening back was often humbling. What I believed sounded polished frequently revealed issues I couldn't perceive while I was in the middle of playing. This one habit accelerated my improvement faster than anything else I did that summer.

04

Listen to Elite Players Daily 🎧

Every morning I listened intentionally — not as background music — to James Galway, Emmanuel Pahud, and Jasmine Choi. Your child's ear needs a vivid, accurate picture of what truly excellent playing sounds like before their hands can begin to replicate it. This is the most underrated, most overlooked habit in middle school band.

"These four habits — nothing more, nothing less — took me from 10th chair to First Chair, All-County, All-District, and a performance stage in Europe. All within a single school year."
```
