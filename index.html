6th

6th Grade: The Squeaks Were Unreal 😬

I picked up the flute in 6th grade and immediately regretted it. The squeaks were constant. My embouchure — the shape your lips make to produce sound — was completely wrong, and I had zero clue how to fix it. Everyone around me was playing actual notes. I was making sounds that made people flinch.

Here's what nobody tells you when you start: flute is one of the hardest instruments to get your first real sound on. Your lip angle, position, and air speed all have to sync up perfectly at the same time. Until they do, you squeak — in front of everyone, every single day.

I almost quit. For real. But I gave myself one more month. And in that month, I cracked it. That was the moment that changed everything.

7th

7th Grade: 10th Chair. The Real Wake-Up. 😤

Chair auditions, 7th grade. I checked the list. 10th chair. Dead last. Out of every flute player in my section, I ranked last. I'm not gonna pretend that didn't sting.

But instead of being upset, I turned that list into a to-do list. 10th chair told me my exact weak spots: inconsistent sight-reading, weak high notes, no control over my dynamics at all. I wrote every weakness down. Then I started eliminating them, one by one.

Here's what I learned: your chair placement isn't a judgment on you. It's a precise map of exactly what to fix next. I'll show you how to use it the exact same way I did.

☀️

The Summer I Actually Locked In 🔒

Between 7th and 8th grade, I made one real decision: no more random practice. Every day, I ran the same four-step system:

  • 20 focused minutes every single day — no skips, no excuses, phone in another room.
  • Recorded every session — your brain lies about how good you sound. A recording never does.
  • Drilled my worst notes first — slowly, perfectly, until they stopped being scary.
  • Listened to pro flutists daily — you literally can't play what you've never truly heard at a high level.

That whole system is free in the Summer Lock-In section below. Don't skip it.

Summer Lock-In

The Plan That Changed Everything.

10th chair → First Chair → All-County → All-District → Europe. All in one summer. Here is every single thing I did, step by step.

The summer between 7th and 8th grade I made myself one real promise: this summer is actually different. Not "I'll practice when I feel motivated." Truly locked in, every day, with a plan. I came back in August and could feel that something real had changed — in my sound, my confidence, and the way the flute felt in my hands. These four things made it happen.

01

20 Minutes. Every. Single. Day. ⏰

Not optional. Weekends. Vacation days. Days you genuinely don't feel like it. Twenty minutes. Phone in another room. Full focus. No exceptions.

Twenty focused daily minutes completely destroys two hours on a weekend, every single time. Your brain builds musical muscle memory through daily repetition — not big occasional bursts. Skip days and you start undoing your own progress.

My split: 5 min long tones · 5 min scales · 5 min your piece · 5 min your worst weakness.

02

Attack Your Worst Spot First. 🎯

Most people practice what they're already decent at because it feels good. That's not progress — that's just staying comfortable. Real improvement only lives in the uncomfortable places.

Every week I picked my single worst weakness and attacked it every session until it stopped being a weakness. High D cracking? Attacked it. G-flat not speaking? Attacked it. Passage falling apart at tempo? Attacked it. One problem per week. Stack the wins. That's the actual formula.

03

Record Yourself Every Session. 📱

This was the biggest game-changer and the most surprising one. Here's the truth: your brain literally lies to you while you play. It fills in what you expect to hear instead of what's actually happening. You think you sound solid. You hit record, play it back, and hear a completely different story.

Use your phone. Record a full run-through every session. Listen back like you're a judge hearing someone else. Fix what you hear. Record again. This one habit alone makes you improve faster than almost anything else.

04

Listen to the Best Players Every Day. 🎧

Every day that summer, I listened to world-class flutists — James Galway, Emmanuel Pahud, Jasmine Choi. Not as background noise. Active, focused listening — paying real attention to their tone, their breath, how they shape every single phrase.

Your ears need a target before your hands can aim at it. You literally cannot produce a sound you've never truly heard at a high level. Ten focused minutes of listening daily is one of the most underrated things on this entire list.
