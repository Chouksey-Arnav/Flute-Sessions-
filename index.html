6th

6th Grade: The Squeaks Were Unreal 😬

I picked up the flute in 6th grade and immediately felt overwhelmed. The squeaks were relentless. My embouchure was completely wrong, and I had no idea how to fix it. Every day I'd sit in band while everyone around me played real notes.

Here's what nobody tells beginners: the flute is genuinely one of the hardest instruments to get your first clean tone on. Lip angle, position, and airspeed all have to line up perfectly. Until they do, you squeak. And that's okay.

I nearly quit. But I gave myself one more month — and I cracked it. If you're feeling exactly like I felt: please keep going. That breakthrough is so much closer than it seems.

🔓 6th Grade Unlocks Consistent tone → Chair audition confidence → First competition eligibility

7th Grade: 10th Out of 16 — My Wake-Up Call 😤

Chair auditions, 7th grade. I checked the posted list: 10th chair out of 16 flutists. That placement showed me exactly where I stood.

But here's what I learned: that number wasn't a sentence. It was a map. Every weakness became a target. Every challenge became a step toward mastery.

If you're a 7th grader staring at a chair number that disappointed you — I see you. That number is not your ceiling. It's your starting point. Let me show you how to use it.

Summer Lock-In

The Plan That Changed Everything.

10th of 16 → First Chair → All-County → All-District → Europe. One summer. Here is everything I did — adapted for every grade.

The summer between 7th and 8th grade, I made one real promise: this summer is actually different. Every single day, with a plan. When I came back in August I could genuinely feel something had shifted. These four pillars made that happen. I believe in you. Let's build this together.

01

Daily Practice — Every. Single. Day. ⏰

Non-negotiable. Weekends. Vacation days. The days you really don't feel like it. Phone in another room. Full presence.

Daily consistency destroys occasional long sessions every time.

🌟 5th Grade: 10 min — 5 breath games · 5 headjoint tone
💚 6th Grade: 15 min — 5 tone · 5 notes · 5 worst spot
💙 7th Grade: 20 min — 5 scales · 5 sight-read · 5 piece · 5 weakness
⭐ 8th Grade: 20 min — 5 long tones · 5 scales · 5 piece · 5 solo artistry
02

Attack Your Worst Spot First. 🎯

Most players practice what they're already decent at because it feels comfortable. Real mastery lives entirely in the uncomfortable places.

Every week I identified my single worst weakness and drilled it every session. One problem per week. Stack the wins.

03

Record Yourself Every Session. 📱

The biggest game-changer. Your brain lies to you while you play. It fills in the sound you expect to hear, not what's actually coming out.

Use your phone. Record. Listen back like a judge hearing a stranger. Fix it. Record again.

04

Listen to the Best Players Every Day. 🎧

Every day that summer I listened to world-class flutists — Galway, Pahud, Jasmine Choi. Not as background noise. Intentional, focused listening.

Your ears need a clear target before your hands can aim at one.

Is this really completely free? There's no hidden cost?

100% free. Every single thing — video sessions, custom practice plans, the summer guide, email Q&A, all tutorials. I am an 8th grader. I have no interest in your money. What I want is for students around me to grow. That's the whole deal.

I'm in 5th grade and haven't started band yet. Is this for me?

Absolutely! The Pre-Band Prep plan is built specifically for 5th graders. Zero experience is actually the perfect starting point. There are no bad habits to undo, and I can make sure you build everything correctly from day one. The Beginner Fast-Track is literally designed for this. I started as a complete beginner too — I know every single hurdle.
