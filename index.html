6th

6th Grade: The Squeaks Were Unreal 😬

I picked up the flute in 6th grade and immediately felt overwhelmed. The squeaks were relentless. My embouchure — the precise lip shape that makes sound possible — was completely wrong, and I had no idea how to fix it. Every day I'd sit in band while everyone around me played real notes.

Here's what nobody tells 6th graders when they start: the flute is genuinely one of the hardest instruments to get your first clean tone on. It's not just you. Lip angle, position, and airspeed all have to line up perfectly at the exact same moment. Until they do, you squeak. And that's okay.

I nearly quit. But I gave myself one more month, and in that month, I cracked it. If you're in 6th grade feeling exactly like I felt: please keep going. That breakthrough is so much closer than it seems.

🔓 6th Grade Unlocks Consistent tone → Chair audition confidence → First competition eligibility
7th

7th Grade: 10th Out of 16 — My Wake-Up Call 😤

Chair auditions, 7th grade. I checked the posted list: 10th chair out of 16 flutists. That placement stung in a way I hadn't expected — and it showed me exactly where I stood.

But here's what I learned: that number wasn't a sentence. It was a map. 10th out of 16 told me precisely what was broken — my sight-reading, my high notes, my dynamics. Every single weakness became a target. Every challenge became a step toward mastery.

If you're a 7th grader staring at a chair number that disappointed you — I see you, and I want you to know: that number is not your ceiling. It's your starting point. Let me show you how to use it exactly the way I did.

🔓 7th Grade Unlocks Chair improvements → All-County → All-District → All-State pathway

Summer Lock-In

The Plan That Changed Everything.

10th of 16 → First Chair → All-County → All-District → Europe. One summer. Here is everything I did — adapted for every grade, ready for you right now.

The summer between 7th and 8th grade, I made one real promise to myself: this summer is actually different. No more "I'll practice when the mood strikes." Every single day, with a plan, locked in. When I came back in August I could genuinely feel something had shifted — in my sound, my confidence, and the way the flute felt like a natural extension of myself. These four pillars made that happen. And they will work for you too, whether you're heading into 6th, 7th, or 8th grade. I believe in you. Now let's build this together.

01

Daily Practice — Every. Single. Day. ⏰

Non-negotiable. Weekends. Vacation days. The days you really, really don't feel like it. Phone in another room. Full presence. No exceptions, because the days you least want to practice are often the days you gain the most.

Daily consistency destroys occasional long sessions every single time. Your brain builds musical muscle memory through repetition — not through bursts. Skip days and you quietly reverse your own progress. Don't let that happen.

💚 6th Grade: 15 min — 5 tone · 5 notes · 5 worst spot
💙 7th Grade: 20 min — 5 scales · 5 sight-read · 5 piece · 5 weakness
⭐ 8th Grade: 20 min — 5 long tones · 5 scales · 5 piece · 5 solo artistry
02

Attack Your Worst Spot First. 🎯

Most players practice what they're already decent at because it feels comfortable and rewarding. That is not growth. Real mastery lives entirely in the uncomfortable places.

Every week I identified my single worst weakness and drilled it every session until it stopped being a weakness. High D cracking? Attacked it. G-flat refusing to speak cleanly? Attacked it. One problem per week. Stack the wins. That is genuinely the formula.

03

Record Yourself Every Session. 📱

This single habit was the biggest game-changer of my entire journey. The truth is: your brain lies to you while you play. It fills in the sound you expect to hear, not what's actually coming out of the instrument.

Use your phone. Record one full run-through per session. Listen back as if you're a judge hearing a total stranger. Fix what you hear. Record again. This feedback loop accelerates your improvement faster than almost anything else on this list.

04

Listen to the Best Players Every Day. 🎧

Every day that summer, I actively listened to world-class flutists — James Galway, Emmanuel Pahud, Jasmine Choi. Not as background noise. Intentional, focused listening — paying close attention to tone quality, breath, phrasing, and how they shape each phrase with care.

Your ears need a clear target before your hands can aim at one. You genuinely cannot produce a sound you've never truly heard at the highest level. Ten minutes of active daily listening is quietly one of the most powerful things on this list.
