The Practice Framework

The Four Habits That Produced Real Results.

These are the exact principles that took Arnav from 10th chair to First Chair, All-County, and Europe — in a single summer.

01

Consistent Daily Practice ⏰

Short, focused sessions every single day — no exceptions. Research consistently shows that consistency outperforms duration.

🌟 5th: 10 min
💚 6th: 15 min
💙 7th: 20 min
⭐ 8th: 20–25 min
02

Target Weaknesses First 🎯

Students naturally gravitate toward what they already do well. Addressing the weakest area at the start of every session — before fatigue sets in — is where real progress happens.

03

Record & Self-Assess 📱

Recording each practice session provides objective feedback that self-perception alone cannot. Students hear what they actually played — not what they intended to play.

04

Active Listening Daily 🎧

Galway. Pahud. Jasmine Choi. Deliberate, attentive listening to elite performers trains the ear to recognize what excellent tone and phrasing actually sound like.

"These four habits, applied consistently, produced First Chair, All-County, All-District, and a performance in Europe — all within one school year."

For Students & Parents

Questions We Know You Have.

Honest, detailed answers — because your family deserves to know exactly what this is before reaching out.

Is this genuinely free — no fees, no subscriptions, no upsells?

Completely free, with no conditions attached. No sign-up fees, no subscription tiers, no "premium" unlock. I'm a high-achieving 8th grader who benefited enormously from focused practice guidance — and I want to give that same advantage to other students at no cost to their families. Your child will never be asked to pay for anything.

Who is Arnav, and why should I trust him with my child's musical development?

I'm Arnav Chouksey, an 8th grader at Mills Park Middle School in Apex, NC. In one year I went from 10th chair out of 16 to First Chair, All-County Honors, All-District Honors, Pre-MPA soloist, and was selected for the NC Bandmasters Association Europe performance trip — one of the highest honors available to middle school band students in North Carolina. I document every technique I used and teach only what I can personally verify worked. Parents are always welcome to sit in on sessions or reach out directly with questions.

Is this appropriate for my child's age and skill level?

Yes — I work with students across all skill levels from 5th through 8th grade. For 5th graders who haven't started band yet, I offer a Pre-Band Prep plan that builds breathing fundamentals and basic tone before school begins, giving them a meaningful head-start. For advanced 8th graders preparing for All-State or high school auditions, I cover expression, phrasing, and competitive strategy. Every session is personalized to your child's exact stage. Sessions are conducted over video call in a focused, respectful, age-appropriate environment.

How does a session actually work? What should we expect?

Sessions are 30 minutes over video call (Zoom, Google Meet, or FaceTime — whatever works best for your family). Your child plays for me, I identify specific areas to improve, and we work through targeted exercises together. At the end of every session I provide a written practice plan your child can follow on their own until our next meeting. I'm available most weekends and some weekday evenings. Simply email me at [email protected] and we'll find a time that works.

My child is already taking private lessons. Can they still benefit?

Absolutely — and many students find this complements their existing lessons well. I focus specifically on the middle school band experience: chair audition strategy, All-County and All-District preparation, sight-reading for school assessments, and the mindset that drives real improvement. A private instructor teaches technique; I teach the competitive and motivational side of middle school band from someone who just lived through it successfully. There's no conflict — only additional support.

What if my child isn't making progress or the fit isn't right?

Because there's no cost involved, there's never any pressure to continue. If at any point you feel it isn't working for your child, you simply stop — no cancellations, no awkward conversations, no obligation of any kind. I genuinely want this to be valuable for your family, and if it's not the right fit, I'll happily recommend other resources. Your child's progress comes first, always.
