6th

6th Grade: The Squeaks Were Unreal 😬

I picked up the flute in 6th grade and immediately questioned everything. The squeaks were relentless. My embouchure — the precise lip shape that produces sound — was completely wrong, and I had zero idea how to correct it. Everyone around me was playing actual notes while I made sounds that made people wince.

Here's what nobody tells you at the start: the flute is one of the hardest instruments to get your first real tone on. Lip angle, position, and airspeed all have to sync up perfectly at the same moment. Until they do, you squeak — in front of everyone, every day.

I almost quit. For real. But I gave myself one more month. In that month, I cracked it. That was the turning point that changed my entire trajectory.

7th

7th Grade: 10th Out of 16. My Real Wake-Up. 😤

Chair auditions, 7th grade. I checked the list. 10th chair out of 16 flutists. Not last, but far from where I wanted to be — and I'm not going to pretend that didn't sting.

But instead of feeling sorry for myself, I turned that placement into a precise to-do list. Being 10th out of 16 was a wake-up call that showed me my exact weak spots — inconsistent sight-reading, thin high notes, no dynamic control — and it fueled my drive to climb to the top.

Every challenge was a step toward mastery. Your chair placement isn't a verdict on your potential — it's a map of exactly what to fix. I'll show you how to use it exactly the way I did.

☀️

The Summer I Decided to Be Great 🔒

Between 7th and 8th grade, I made one real decision: no more unfocused practice. Every day, I ran the same four-step system:

  • 20 focused minutes every single day — no skips, no excuses, phone in another room.
  • Recorded every session — your brain lies about how good you sound. A recording never does.
  • Drilled my worst notes first — slowly, perfectly, until they stopped being scary.
  • Listened to pro flutists daily — you cannot play what you've never truly heard at a high level.

That complete system is waiting for you in the Summer Lock-In section below. Don't skip it.

Summer Lock-In

The Plan That Changed Everything.

10th out of 16 → First Chair → All-County → All-District → Europe. All in one summer. Here is every single thing I did, step by step.

The summer between 7th and 8th grade I made myself one real promise: this summer is actually different. Not "I'll practice when I feel motivated." Truly locked in, every single day, with intention. I came back in August and could feel that something had genuinely shifted — in my sound, my confidence, and the way the flute felt like an extension of myself. These four pillars made it happen.

01

20 Minutes. Every. Single. Day. ⏰

Not optional. Weekends. Vacation days. Days you genuinely don't feel like it. Twenty minutes. Phone in another room. Full focus. No exceptions.

Twenty focused daily minutes demolishes two hours once a weekend, every time. Your brain builds musical muscle memory through consistent daily repetition — not big occasional bursts. Skip days and you start reversing your own gains.

My split: 5 min long tones · 5 min scales · 5 min your piece · 5 min your single worst weakness.

02

Attack Your Worst Spot First. 🎯

Most players practice what they're already decent at because it feels good. That's not growth — that's staying comfortable. Real mastery lives in the uncomfortable places.

Every week I identified my single worst weakness and attacked it every session until it ceased to be a weakness. High D cracking? Attacked it. G-flat refusing to speak? Attacked it. Passage collapsing at tempo? Attacked it. One problem per week. Stack the victories. That's the actual formula.

03

Record Yourself Every Session. 📱

This was the single biggest game-changer. Here's the truth: your brain lies to you while you play. It fills in what you expect to hear rather than what's actually coming out. You think you sound solid. You hit record, play it back, and hear a completely different story.

Use your phone. Record one full run-through per session. Listen back as though you're a judge hearing a stranger. Fix what you hear. Record again. This loop alone accelerates improvement faster than almost anything else on this list.

04

Listen to the Best Players Every Day. 🎧

Every day that summer, I listened to world-class flutists — James Galway, Emmanuel Pahud, Jasmine Choi. Not as background noise. Active, intentional listening — paying close attention to tone, breath, and how they sculpt every phrase.

Your ears need a target before your hands can aim at one. You cannot produce a sound you have never truly heard at the highest level. Ten minutes of focused daily listening is quietly one of the most powerful habits on this entire list.
